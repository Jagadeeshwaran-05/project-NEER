# Lake Water Quality Guidance Notes

## BOD interpretation (practical thresholds)
- BOD <= 4 mg/L: generally good condition.
- BOD > 4 and <= 8 mg/L: moderate stress; active monitoring needed.
- BOD > 8 mg/L: poor condition; immediate mitigation is recommended.

## NDWI and water stress
- Low NDWI can indicate reduced surface water extent or stressed water body conditions.
- Persistently low NDWI should trigger checks on inflow, encroachment, and desilting needs.

## Turbidity and sediment
- High turbidity is commonly linked to catchment erosion, stormwater inflow, construction runoff, and waste dumping.
- Actions: silt traps, shoreline vegetation buffers, and enforcement against dumping.

## Algal bloom indicators
- Elevated chlorophyll-related indices (e.g., NDCI/FAI patterns) may suggest eutrophication.
- Actions: nutrient source control, sewage interception, and periodic bloom monitoring.

## Priority action template
1. Immediate (0-30 days): identify and stop dominant pollution inflow.
2. Short term (1-6 months): reduce sewage and sediment loads.
3. Long term (6+ months): restore buffers, maintain periodic monitoring, and publish trends.

## Communication guidance
- Recommendations should be practical and measurable.
- Mention uncertainty when metrics are borderline.
- Prefer concise responses with clear next actions.
